# Report on “AWS First Cloud AI Journey Community Day – May 23, 2026”

### Purpose of the Event

+ Update participants on the latest trends in Artificial Intelligence (AI), Cloud Computing, and AWS solutions.
+ Share practical experiences in applying AI to businesses and software development.
+ Introduce AWS services that support data analytics, performance optimization, and AI-powered systems.
+ Connect students with industry experts, enterprises, and the technology community.

### Speakers

+ Tinh Truong – AI Context Engineering and Second AI Brain.
+ Anh Pham – Introduction to Amazon Quick Suite.
+ Thinh Nguyen – Amazon CloudFront.
+ Team VIB – Presenting the UTMorpho project from LotusHacks.
+ Duc Dao – The Non-Determinism of Large Language Models.
+ Vy Lam – Enterprise-Grade Multi-Agent Systems for Credit Scoring.

### Key Highlights

#### Context Is Everything: Making AI Actually Work for You

+ Explained why AI often produces poor results when sufficient context is not provided.
+ Introduced the concept of the Second AI Brain and the role of memory in AI systems.
+ Shared practical techniques for writing prompts and providing effective context.
+ Offered career guidance and learning strategies for students pursuing AI.

#### Friendly AI Assistant with Amazon Quick

+ Introduced Amazon Quick Chat for natural language data exploration.
+ Demonstrated Quick Flows for creating AI-powered workflows without coding.
+ Showed how Quick Spaces enable collaborative knowledge sharing.
+ Explained how QuickSight creates dashboards and reports directly from raw data.

#### From Edge To Origin: CloudFront as Your Foundation

+ Explained the role of Amazon CloudFront in global content delivery.
+ Shared cost optimization strategies for CDN deployments.
+ Introduced built-in security features such as SSL, AWS Shield, and AWS WAF.
+ Demonstrated how CloudFront improves application performance and availability.

#### 36 Hours with LotusHacks – Building UTMorpho

+ Shared the journey of building a product during a 36-hour Hackathon.
+ Explained the process from brainstorming to developing an MVP.
+ Discussed challenges, failures, and how the team overcame pressure.
+ Demonstrated the final product and shared key lessons learned.

#### Non-Determinism of "Deterministic" LLM Settings

+ Explained how Large Language Models generate the next token.
+ Discussed why setting Temperature = 0 does not always guarantee identical outputs.
+ Described the impact of inference optimizations on model behavior.
+ Presented practical strategies to reduce non-deterministic results.

#### Enterprise-Grade Multi-Agent System

+ Analyzed the limitations of traditional banking systems in evaluating startups.
+ Compared Single-Agent and Multi-Agent architectures.
+ Introduced the Virtual Credit Committee architecture.
+ Discussed Guardrails, Compliance, and practical implementation roadmaps.

### What I Learned

#### AI Knowledge

+ Understood the importance of Context Engineering in improving AI response quality.
+ Learned how to write effective prompts and leverage memory to enhance AI performance.
+ Understood why LLMs may still produce non-deterministic outputs even under identical settings.

#### AWS Cloud Knowledge

+ Learned how Amazon CloudFront improves website performance, reduces latency, and enhances security.
+ Gained an understanding of Amazon Quick Suite and its capabilities for data analytics and workflow automation.
+ Learned how multiple AWS services can be combined to build modern AI solutions.

#### Product Development Experience

+ Learned the end-to-end product development process through a Hackathon.
+ Understood the importance of teamwork, task allocation, and time management.
+ Learned how to transform an idea into a working MVP within a limited timeframe.

#### Enterprise AI Architecture

+ Learned when to use Single-Agent and Multi-Agent architectures.
+ Understood how to design AI systems with scalability and security in mind.
+ Learned the importance of Guardrails for controlling AI behavior.

#### Practical Applications

+ Apply Context Engineering and Prompt Engineering techniques when using AI for learning and software development.
+ Use Amazon CloudFront to optimize website performance and security on AWS.
+ Explore Amazon Quick Suite for data analytics and business intelligence projects.
+ Consider Multi-Agent architectures when building AI applications that require collaboration among multiple intelligent agents.
+ Apply Hackathon experiences in time management and MVP development to future personal projects.

### Event Experience

Participating in this event allowed me to explore the latest trends in Artificial Intelligence and Cloud Computing through presentations delivered by AWS experts and industry professionals. I learned how AI becomes significantly more effective when provided with sufficient context and gained hands-on knowledge of AWS services such as Amazon Quick Suite and Amazon CloudFront.

The sessions on Large Language Models and Multi-Agent Systems provided deeper insights into how modern AI operates and how these technologies are implemented in enterprise environments. In particular, Team VIB's presentation on developing UTMorpho within a 36-hour Hackathon offered valuable lessons in teamwork, problem-solving, and rapid product development under tight deadlines.

The event also provided an excellent opportunity to connect with experts and members of the technology community, helping me broaden my knowledge, gain career insights, and stay updated on the latest developments in AI and Cloud Computing.

### Lessons Learned

+ AI delivers the best results only when it is provided with sufficient context and high-quality data.
+ Amazon CloudFront plays a critical role in improving application performance, security, and scalability.
+ Amazon Quick Suite simplifies data analytics and AI assistant development through natural language interactions.
+ Large Language Models are not completely deterministic even with Temperature = 0, making it important to understand the inference process when building AI applications.
+ Multi-Agent Systems provide an effective solution for complex enterprise scenarios that require multiple AI agents to collaborate.
+ The Hackathon experience demonstrated that effective time management, strong teamwork, and focusing on building a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) are essential factors for successful product development.
